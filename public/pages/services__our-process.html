How We Work — Step by Step

01

Step 1
Consultation & Inventory Assessment
Every project begins with a detailed consultation. We assess your inventory, evaluate access conditions at origin and destination, identify special handling requirements, and document the full scope of work. For larger or institutional projects, we conduct on-site evaluations.

02

Step 2
Compliance Review & Planning
Our operations team reviews each project for regulatory requirements — including CPUC regulations, building access rules, HOA restrictions, parking permits, and any institutional or program-specific compliance standards. For government and contract-based engagements, we verify adherence to program guidelines and documentation requirements.

03

Step 3
Pricing & Resource Allocation
Based on the project scope, our pricing team calculates costs using seasonal demand models, distance-based factors, crew and equipment requirements, and contract-specific parameters. Resources are allocated — including crew size, truck type, specialized equipment, and packing materials — to match the operational plan.

04

Step 4
Execution
On move day, our trained crews arrive on time with all necessary equipment and materials. Every item is protected, loaded, transported, and placed according to the operational plan. Communication is maintained with the client throughout the process.

05

Step 5
Delivery & Completion Confirmation
At the destination, all items are placed per client instructions. Furniture is reassembled, boxes are positioned in designated rooms, and a final walkthrough is conducted with the client. For institutional projects, completion documentation is prepared and submitted per contract requirements.

Pricing & Operational Analysis

SOS Moving employs a data-driven approach to pricing and operational planning across all project types:

Seasonal Pricing Models

Our rates reflect demand patterns across peak and off-peak periods, ensuring competitive pricing while maintaining service capacity and crew availability.

Cost Calculation Per B2B Project & Institutional Contract

Each commercial and institutional project receives individual cost analysis based on scope, complexity, timeline, and compliance requirements.

Resource Allocation & Operational Cost Modeling

We model resource needs — crew hours, truck utilization, fuel, materials, and equipment — to optimize efficiency and control costs on every project.

Profitability & Efficiency Analysis

Each project type is analyzed for operational efficiency, allowing continuous improvement in service delivery and resource management.
